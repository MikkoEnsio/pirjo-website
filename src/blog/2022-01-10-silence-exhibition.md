Silence can be a strong psychophysical presence, emptiness or absence of someone or something. It can be a short break or a delicate moment before something disappears or ends, a short moment of calm when the house wakes up to everyday life or a Sunday morning's lingering joy.

In the studio, before colleagues arrive, the gaze finds the movements and details of the light that can only be noticed in silence.

The silence lies in the melancholy end of summer life with its hustle and bustle, the change of seasons and in the cycle of life.

Silence is not only a lack of sound, it is also full of beginnings and endings of stories.
